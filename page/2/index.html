User-centric design care about more details of users and their habits. Human-centric design emphasizes the importance of the human experience in solving problems.

UX and UI are closely related.

UX design is always conducted in first step in producing process, before the step of UI design. UX designers draw user journey maps and basic framework of the users which will be improved with visual and interactive elements by UI designers.

Since UX performed before UI, we need to solve the problem by using people-oriented design. Designers must firmly grasp the requirements of users. They have to ask for user experience questions.

Designers need to build the best user experience for getting a good user interface.

Design thinking is an iterative process in the creative problem-solving process.

Design thinking is important because it helps to create the right environment for a practical and broad understanding of the customer’s voice.

Empathize, the first stages of Design thinking process.

The first stage of the design process is to understand the perspective of the target audience/customer/consumer to identify and address the problem at hand.

Asking yourself these three questions is a good way to start practicing empathy in your everyday interactions:

  • Can you identify this feeling?
  • How would you describe about felt this way?

A journey map is a synthetic representation that describes a step-by-step description of how a user uses a service.

journey map is the process of providing a complete picture of the customer experience by revealing moments of both frustration and joy through a series of interactions. User Interface and User Experience Week 4

2021-04-27

this week we mainly learnt about the Design Principles and Guidelines.

Intro

  • Design principles: High-level, context-free design goals based on human-computer interaction theory.
  • Design guidelines: The specific and generally context-dependent rules that designers follow to implement these principles.
  • Design Standards: standards are to ensure quality

Differences

Design principles Design guidelines
Improve users’ task performance and reduce their effort. Maintain consistent interaction.
Strive for fit between the information representation needed and presented Provide the user with control over the interaction, supported by feedback.
Direct and constrain user affordances to capture real world knowledge Use metaphors.
Design for error. Use direct manipulation

Principles tend to be very general and would apply pretty much across different technologies and systems.

Guidelines tend to be more specific to a device or system.

Section B

Describe the mental model of groceries list to buy. List out each step you take to accomplish the goal.

Experience:
Regarding when people go for shopping in mental experience, people get in the shop to buy the things they in their plan or to just look around and to buy something randomly. They are shopping with a container, their hands, baskets or carts, to keep the item they choose to buy. they are walking around the shop to find the item which they want to buy, and the items will be put in container once they find it after they check the information. At the end of shopping, before they leave, they have to pay the money according to the items.

Then we put it into mental model:
Step 1. Browse and search items
Step 2. Find out more information of the items
Step 3. Add/Remove items to/from the shopping cart
Step 4. Checkout
Step 5. Choose how to pay and a shipping/delivery option
Step 6. submit order
Step 7. Get some type of feedback confirming the order was done
Step 8. The shopping process is done

Metaphor

In user interface design, an interface metaphor is a set of user interface visuals, actions, behaviours and procedures that exploit specific knowledge that users already have of other areas, in order to help users to interact with the user interface.

Verbal metaphor

Knowledge about a familiar domain in terms of elements and their relation to each other is mapped on to those in the unfamiliar one

Virtual metaphor

To create electronic counterparts to the physical objects in an office.

Verbal metaphor

Combine two metaphors or more together for carrying out a range of computer based actions

Outline

  1. What is cognition?
  2. Describe how cognition has been applied to interaction design
  3. Explain what are Mental Models

What is cognition

Cognition refers to what goes on in humans mind when they carry out everyday activities. It involves lots of cognitive processes, such as thinking, remembering, learning and decision making.

Design Implication Aspects

Attention

make user to FOCUS on IMPORTANTs

Make information salient when it needs attending to

Perception

easy to DISTINGUISH and READ

The implication is to design representations that are readily perceivable

Memory

make it being RECOGNIZED, not recalled

We recognize things much better than being able to recall things

Learning

make it EASY to get in

Design interfaces that constrain and guide learners

Reading, speaking and listening

should be CLEAR and ACCURATE

Problem-solving, planning, reasoning and decision-making

Provide additional information/functions for users who wish to understand more about how to carry out an activity more effectively

Mental model

Operation ways affected by real world

A mental model is an explanation of someone’s thought process about how something works in the real world.

dir - Displaying Directory Content

cd [dir-name] - change to the [dir-name] directory

dir . - Displaying current directory content

dir .. - Displaying parent directory content

dir [file-name] - Displaying current directory content

rd [directory_name] - Delete a void directory
